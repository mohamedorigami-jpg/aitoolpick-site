Which Tool Should You Start With?

If you only pick one tool, pick Rytr. It handles the highest-volume writing task for most agents - property descriptions and client emails - and the free plan gives you enough to test it properly before spending anything.

If your agency has a website and you want to grow organic traffic, add Koala at around £7/month and commit to publishing one area guide or advice article per week. Local SEO is one of the few channels where independent agents can genuinely compete with national portals, and content is the way to do it.

For agencies spending time on social media and printed marketing materials, Canva Pro at £99/year pays for itself quickly. The Brand Kit alone is worth it for anyone producing more than a few pieces of content per week.

NeuronWriter is worth adding once you are publishing content regularly and want to ensure every piece is properly optimised before it goes live. ChatGPT Plus is a good complement to any of these tools for the tasks that don't fit neatly into a template.

What About Specialist Estate Agency AI Tools?

There are a number of estate agency-specific AI platforms emerging in the UK market, including Street.co.uk and Alto's AI-enhanced CRM features. These are more expensive and better suited to larger agencies managing hundreds of listings. For independent agents and small chains, the general-purpose tools listed above offer better value and more flexibility.

If you are running a larger operation and want to evaluate specialist proptech AI, look at Street.co.uk, Reapit, and Dezrez, all of which have added AI features to their CRM platforms in 2025 and 2026.
