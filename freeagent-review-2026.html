What Is FreeAgent?

FreeAgent was founded in 2007 in Edinburgh. It was built from the start for UK freelancers and contractors who needed to handle their own accounts without hiring a full-time bookkeeper or spending hours inside a spreadsheet.

The software covers the main financial tasks a small business deals with every month. You can create and send invoices, track expenses, connect your bank account, run payroll for a small team, and prepare your Self Assessment or corporation tax return. Everything feeds into a single set of accounts. You do not need to export data to another tool or manually reconcile figures at year end.

In 2018, NatWest Group acquired FreeAgent. The product stayed independent in terms of its interface and feature development, but the acquisition created the free-for-customers deal that now makes FreeAgent particularly attractive to anyone already banking with NatWest or its digital offshoot, Mettle.

FreeAgent currently serves more than 150,000 small businesses and has a strong position among IT contractors operating through limited companies, as well as sole traders and freelancers across design, writing, consulting, and other professional services.

Key Features

Invoicing

FreeAgent's invoicing tool is clean and practical. You can create branded invoices in a few minutes, set payment terms, and add automatic payment reminders that go out when an invoice goes overdue. When a client pays and the payment shows up in your bank feed, FreeAgent matches it to the invoice automatically. For freelancers who invoice regularly, this alone removes a meaningful chunk of admin.

You can also set up recurring invoices for retainer clients, which is useful if you have clients on monthly arrangements. The invoice PDF looks professional out of the box, though customisation options are limited compared to dedicated invoicing tools.

Expense Tracking

Expenses can be logged via the mobile app by photographing receipts. The app reads the amount and date, and you assign a category. This works well for contractors who travel or have regular out-of-pocket costs. FreeAgent also has a mileage tracker so you can log business journeys and claim the HMRC approved rate.

The categorisation system maps to the categories HMRC expects on a Self Assessment or corporation tax return, which means less work at year end reconciling your bookkeeping categories against your tax return categories.

Bank Feeds and Reconciliation

FreeAgent connects to most UK banks via Open Banking. Transactions import automatically and you can match them to invoices, assign them to expense categories, or mark them as transfers. The interface is straightforward and most freelancers get comfortable with the reconciliation process within their first few weeks of use.

If your bank does not support Open Banking yet, you can import transactions manually via CSV. It is more time-consuming but functional.

Self Assessment (SA)

This is where FreeAgent genuinely earns its subscription for sole traders. Throughout the year, as you log income and expenses, FreeAgent builds your Self Assessment return in the background. By the time January rolls around, most of the return is already populated from your bookkeeping data. You review the figures, check them against your records, and file directly to HMRC through FreeAgent's HMRC connection.

For freelancers who previously paid an accountant £150 to £300 to file a straightforward Self Assessment, this feature can pay for itself several times over in the first year.

VAT Returns (Making Tax Digital)

FreeAgent is fully compatible with Making Tax Digital (MTD) for VAT. If you are VAT registered, your VAT return figures calculate automatically from your records and you can file directly to HMRC from inside FreeAgent. For anyone who has previously had to export figures to bridging software or do this manually, the direct MTD filing is a genuine improvement.

Payroll

FreeAgent includes basic payroll for small teams. You can process PAYE, generate payslips, and file Real Time Information (RTI) submissions to HMRC. For a one or two-person limited company with a director salary, this is sufficient. For businesses with more than five or six employees, you will likely want dedicated payroll software. The FreeAgent payroll tool is functional but not feature-rich.

Projects and Time Tracking

FreeAgent lets you create projects, log time against them, and track profitability. For consultants or agencies that bill by the hour or want to understand which clients are most profitable, this is useful. The time tracker is basic compared to dedicated tools like Harvest or Toggl, but it handles most common use cases and feeds directly into invoice generation.

Dashboard and Tax Timeline

The FreeAgent dashboard shows your cash flow position at a glance, including what you owe in tax. The tax timeline feature estimates your upcoming HMRC bills based on your current income, which helps freelancers set aside the right amount throughout the year rather than facing a surprise bill in January. This is one of the features that non-accountant users consistently mention as valuable.

Who Is FreeAgent Best For?

UK freelancers and sole traders

This is the core audience the product was designed for. If you are a freelance writer, designer, developer, consultant, or contractor working as a sole trader, FreeAgent covers everything you need. The Self Assessment filing feature alone justifies the subscription cost for most people in this category, particularly if you have previously paid an accountant to file a straightforward return.

IT contractors through a limited company

FreeAgent has a long history with IT contractors. The limited company plan at £24 per month handles invoicing to agencies or clients, director salary payroll, dividends records, and corporation tax preparation. Many contractors use FreeAgent alongside a specialist contractor accountant who logs in via the shared access feature to handle the year-end filing.

NatWest and Mettle account holders

If you already bank with NatWest or Mettle, using FreeAgent is straightforward. It is free, it connects directly to your account, and the bank feed works reliably. There is no meaningful reason to use a different accounting tool in this situation unless your business needs features FreeAgent does not provide.

Freelancers who want to reduce accountant fees

At £19 to £24 per month, FreeAgent costs £228 to £288 per year. If it replaces even one accountant appointment per year, it has paid for itself. For sole traders with straightforward affairs, FreeAgent plus a brief accountant review at year end is a practical and cost-effective arrangement.

Not ideal: businesses with stock or inventory

FreeAgent has no inventory management. If you hold stock, sell physical products through multiple channels, or need to track cost of goods sold at a granular level, you will need a different tool. Xero with an inventory add-on, or dedicated e-commerce accounting software, would be a better fit.

Not ideal: businesses with complex payroll needs

FreeAgent's payroll works well for small setups, particularly one or two-person limited companies paying a director salary. For businesses with eight or more employees, variable hours, pension contributions across multiple providers, or complex benefit structures, you will run into the limits of the FreeAgent payroll tool fairly quickly.

Not ideal: businesses with significant overseas income

If a meaningful portion of your revenue comes from outside the UK and involves multiple currencies, FreeAgent is not the right tool. Multi-currency support is limited, and the reporting does not handle foreign exchange in a way that suits businesses with complex overseas trading.

How Does FreeAgent Compare to Alternatives?

The main competitors for UK freelancers are Xero, QuickBooks, and Sage. Each covers broadly similar ground on invoicing, expenses, and bank feeds.

Xero starts cheaper (around £16 per month on the Starter plan) but the features that freelancers actually need tend to require the Standard plan at around £33 per month. Xero has a larger integrations library and better reporting, which matters if you are growing. For a straightforward freelance setup, it is more than you need.

QuickBooks Self-Employed is cheaper, starting around £10 per month, but the lower plan does not include VAT returns or limited company accounts. The step up to a plan with comparable features brings the cost in line with FreeAgent.

Sage has stronger payroll and is better suited to businesses with employees, but it is less intuitive for self-employed individuals and the interface has historically required more accounting knowledge to navigate.

FreeAgent's UK-specific focus and the free tier for NatWest and Mettle customers give it a clear advantage for its target audience. If you are a sole trader or small limited company operating entirely in the UK, it is the most purpose-built option at this price point.

Our Verdict

Our Verdict
FreeAgent does exactly what most UK freelancers and small limited company owners need. It handles the compliance work that non-accountants find stressful, files directly with HMRC for both VAT and Self Assessment, and presents your financial position in a way that is genuinely readable without an accounting background. If you bank with NatWest or Mettle, there is no reason not to use it. For everyone else, £19 to £24 per month is a fair price for a tool that was designed for the UK market from the start. The main weaknesses are limited reporting, basic payroll, and no inventory support. If those are not problems your business has, FreeAgent is a strong choice.

The NatWest free deal is the single most compelling reason to consider switching banks if you are not already committed elsewhere. A free business bank account plus free accounting software that connects directly to HMRC is a meaningful saving for anyone in the early stages of freelancing or running a small business.
