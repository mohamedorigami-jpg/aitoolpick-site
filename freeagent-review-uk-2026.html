What Is FreeAgent?

FreeAgent is a cloud-based accounting platform built specifically for UK small businesses, freelancers, and contractors. Unlike many accounting tools that started as US products and added UK compliance later, FreeAgent was designed from the ground up around HMRC requirements. This means Making Tax Digital (MTD) for VAT, Real Time Information (RTI) payroll, and self-assessment integration are standard features rather than add-ons.

The platform covers the full accounting cycle: invoicing, expense tracking, bank reconciliation, VAT returns, payroll, and year-end tax submissions. All figures are in GBP by default, and the dashboard shows your tax liability in real time - no end-of-year surprises. FreeAgent also connects directly to over 40 UK banks for automatic transaction feeds, reducing manual data entry significantly.

Key Features

Invoicing and Estimates

FreeAgent lets you create professional invoices and estimates in minutes. Templates are customisable with your logo and brand colours. You can set up recurring invoices for regular clients, send automated payment reminders, and accept online payments via Stripe or PayPal. The mobile app allows you to create and send estimates from job sites, which is useful for tradespeople and service businesses.

Bank Feeds and Reconciliation

FreeAgent connects to over 40 UK banks including Barclays, NatWest, HSBC, Lloyds, and Monzo. Transactions import automatically each day, and the platform uses machine learning to suggest categories based on your history. Reconciliation is straightforward - you match imported transactions against invoices and bills, and FreeAgent flags any discrepancies. For UK businesses processing high transaction volumes, this can save hours of manual bookkeeping each week.

MTD and VAT

FreeAgent is fully MTD-compatible for VAT. The platform submits VAT returns directly to HMRC through the MTD API. You can run VAT reports at any time to check your liability before submission. Flat Rate Scheme, Annual Accounting, and Cash Accounting schemes are all supported. For 2026, FreeAgent continues to support all MTD phases as HMRC expands the programme.

Payroll

FreeAgent includes RTI payroll for up to 5 employees at no extra cost. You can run payroll each period, auto-calculate deductions, and submit RTI returns directly to HMRC. This is a genuine differentiator - most accounting platforms charge extra for payroll or limit it to higher-tier plans.

Pricing

FreeAgent uses a single plan model with no tiered feature gating, which simplifies decision-making. All prices are in GBP and exclude VAT. The first 6 months are discounted, making it affordable to test during your busiest period.

Our Verdict

THE VERDICT

FreeAgent is the best UK-specific accounting tool for freelancers and micro-businesses

FreeAgent stands out because it was built for the UK market from day one. The MTD compliance, RTI payroll, and real-time tax dashboard are not afterthoughts - they are core features that work well. For freelancers, contractors, and micro-businesses with straightforward accounting needs, FreeAgent is arguably the best value option available in 2026 at £21/month.

However, it is less suitable for larger businesses with complex inventory, multi-currency needs, or international bank accounts. In those cases, Xero or QuickBooks offer more flexibility despite being more expensive and less UK-focused out of the box. For the audience it targets, FreeAgent delivers exceptional value and ease of use.

How FreeAgent Compares

FreeAgent competes directly with Xero and QuickBooks in the UK small business accounting space. Xero starts at around £12/month but charges extra for payroll, project tracking, and multi-currency features. QuickBooks starts at £6/month for the Essential plan but has a steeper learning curve and a less intuitive interface. FreeAgent sits at £21/month with all core features included and no hidden costs for payroll or MTD compliance.

For UK businesses specifically, FreeAgent's main advantage is the depth of its HMRC integrations. Its main disadvantage is the single-plan structure - if you only need basic invoicing and expense tracking, you are still paying the full £21/month. In that scenario, QuickBooks Essential at £6/month may be more cost-effective, though you lose the payroll and MTD features.
