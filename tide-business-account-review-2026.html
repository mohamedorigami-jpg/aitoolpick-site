Tide Business Account Review 2026: Is It Worth It for UK Sole Traders?

Finance & Banking Updated 20 March 2026 8 min read5 tools compared Independently reviewed

Tide is one of the most widely used business bank accounts in the UK, with over 1 million members. It is built for sole traders, freelancers, and small limited companies who want a business account they can open in minutes without walking into a branch. This review covers what you actually get, what it costs in GBP, where it falls short, and whether it is worth it in 2026.

Bottom line up front: Tide is a solid free account for sole traders who want basic banking sorted quickly. If you need cashback, accounting integrations, or dedicated support, the paid plans are worth considering. If you need a full overdraft or in-branch support, look elsewhere.

Key Takeaways

  • Free plan is genuinely usable for low-volume sole traders
  • No phone support on any plan - chat only
  • Strong accounting integrations: FreeAgent, Xero, QuickBooks
  • 20p per transfer on Free plan - upgrade to Plus if you make many payments
  • FSCS protected up to £85,000 via ClearBank

What Is Tide?

Tide is a UK financial technology company that provides mobile-first business bank accounts. It is not a bank in the traditional sense - your deposits are held with ClearBank, which is FSCS protected up to £85,000. Tide itself handles the app, the cards, and the features wrapped around the account.

It launched in 2017 and has grown to serve over 1 million UK businesses, making it one of the largest business account providers in the country alongside Starling and Monzo Business.

Tide Plans and Pricing (GBP, 2026)

Tide has four account tiers. There is also a £1 ATM withdrawal fee on the Free plan. International transfers are handled through a separate fee structure starting at £10 per transfer.

Key Features

Account Opening

You can open a Tide account in under 10 minutes via the app. You need a UK address, a valid ID, and a phone number. Sole traders and limited companies are both eligible. Approval is usually instant, though some applications require manual review.

Invoicing

Tide has a built-in invoicing tool that lets you create and send branded invoices directly from the app. You can match incoming payments to invoices automatically, which saves time chasing. It is not a replacement for dedicated accounting software, but for simple invoicing it works well.

Integrations

Tide connects to FreeAgent, Xero, QuickBooks, and Sage. The FreeAgent integration is particularly useful - you can sync transactions in real time, which makes VAT returns and Self Assessment significantly less painful. Making Tax Digital (MTD) compliance is much easier with this set up.

Expense Management

You can categorise transactions, add receipts via the app camera, and export data as CSV or connect directly to your accounting software. On the Pro plan you get team expense management, which is useful if you have employees or contractors spending on company cards.

Business Loans

Tide has partnered with iwoca to offer business loans up to £500,000 through the app. Approval decisions are fast and you do not need to switch your main account to iwoca. This is one area where Tide has a clear advantage over purely basic accounts.

Customer Support

Support is in-app chat only on the Free plan, with typical response times of a few hours during business days. On Plus and above you get priority support with faster responses. There is no phone support on any plan, which is a dealbreaker for some business owners.

Who Is Tide Best For?

Tide works best for UK sole traders and freelancers who want a business account they can open today and forget about. If you invoice clients, want FreeAgent or Xero integration, and do not need to make dozens of bank transfers per month, the free plan covers most of what you need.

The Plus plan at £9.99/month is worth it the moment you start making more than 50 transfers per month - you will save money compared to paying 20p per transfer on Free.

The Pro and Cashback plans suit small businesses with higher card spend. At £18.99/month you need to spend at least £3,798/month on the card to break even on the 0.5% cashback. At £49.99/month on Cashback you need to spend at least £4,999/month on the card for the 1% to pay off.

Tide vs Starling vs Monzo Business

Starling Business offers a free account with unlimited UK transfers at no extra charge, which makes it more generous than Tide Free for high-volume users. Starling also has better in-app customer support response times and a slightly cleaner interface. However, Tide has more invoicing features and a broader range of paid plans.

Monzo Business is similar to Tide in pricing but has stronger team features and a more polished app experience. For sole traders, the difference is marginal. For small teams, Monzo Business Pro has a slight edge.

Final Verdict

Our Rating
4.1 / 5
Tide is a reliable, well-established business account that gets the basics right. The free plan is good enough for many sole traders. The accounting integrations are strong, the invoicing tool is useful, and the app works well day to day. The main weaknesses are the per-transfer fee on Free, the lack of phone support, and the high cost of the Cashback plan relative to what you get. If you are a UK sole trader or freelancer looking to open a business account today, Tide is one of the first three you should consider alongside Starling and Mettle.
